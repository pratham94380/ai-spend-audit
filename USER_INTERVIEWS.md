# User Interviews

## Overview

To better understand how developers and AI-heavy users currently manage AI tooling subscriptions and costs, I conducted a set of lightweight informal interviews with students, developers, and creative users actively using modern AI tools.

The goal was to understand:
- current AI tooling workflows
- subscription behavior
- awareness of overspending
- willingness to optimize tooling costs

---

# Interview 1 — Creative AI User

**Role:** Creative AI User / Designer  
**Tools Used:** Leonardo AI, Gemini, ChatGPT  
**Estimated Monthly Spend:** ~₹50k combined usage and experimentation budget

### Key Responses

> “I use Leonardo, Gemini, and ChatGPT heavily for different creative workflows.”

> “I spend a lot experimenting with tools and honestly don’t always track the cost properly.”

> “Sometimes I subscribe to tools just to test features and forget about optimization.”

### Insights

- Users often stack multiple AI subscriptions without comparing overlap.
- Creative users value workflow convenience more than cost efficiency.
- Subscription sprawl becomes common when experimenting with AI tools.

### Product Impact

This reinforced the need for:
- multi-tool audit support
- combined spend visibility
- overspending detection logic

---

# Interview 2 — Student  Developer

**Role:** Student Developer  
**Primary Use Case:** Coding and productivity

### Key Responses

> “When I can’t work on what I love, I end up wasting time on tools that don’t really help.”

> “I mainly use AI tools for coding and learning workflows.”

> “I’ve never seriously tracked whether my subscriptions are worth the money.”

### Insights

- Many users lack visibility into actual ROI from subscriptions.
- Emotional/productivity-driven subscriptions are common.
- Users rarely calculate effective value from AI plans.

### Product Impact

This validated:
- recommendation summaries
- plan optimization suggestions
- savings visibility features

---

# Interview 3 — General AI User

**Role:** General AI Power User  
**Primary Use Case:** Mixed productivity and experimentation

### Key Responses

> “Always 😅😅”

(Response when asked whether they feel they overspend on AI tools.)

> “Maybe, I never really tried comparing plans properly.”

### Insights

- Users are aware of overspending but often ignore optimization.
- Most users never benchmark alternative plans or providers.
- There is strong potential for lightweight AI cost optimization tools.

### Product Impact

This influenced:
- simple onboarding design
- low-friction audit workflow
- fast summary generation instead of complex dashboards

---

# Overall Learnings

Across interviews, the biggest patterns were:

- users frequently underestimate total AI spend
- multiple overlapping subscriptions are common
- few users compare plans seriously
- users prefer simple actionable recommendations over complex analytics

The interviews reinforced that AI spend optimization is still a relatively underserved workflow, especially for:
- students
- small startups
- solo developers
- creative AI users

This validated the core direction of building a lightweight AI spend audit platform.
