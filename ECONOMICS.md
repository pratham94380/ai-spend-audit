# Unit Economics

## Core Business Model

AI Spend Audit functions primarily as a lead-generation product for Credex.

The product itself is free, but high-savings audits identify companies likely to benefit from:
- discounted AI infrastructure credits
- tooling optimization
- enterprise procurement support

The value of the product comes from converting high-intent audits into infrastructure credit customers.

---

# Estimated Lead Value

Assumption:

A startup spending heavily on AI tooling may spend:

- $500–$5000/month on AI products
- $6,000–$60,000 annually

If Credex captures:
- 10–20% margin on infrastructure resale or optimization deals

then a converted customer could reasonably generate:

```text
$500–$5000 annual gross value
```

depending on company size and usage intensity.

Assuming:
- average annual value per converted customer = $2,000

Then:

```text
9 customers × $2,000

```

Approximate annualized revenue contribution:

```text
~$216,000/year
```

with modest traffic assumptions.

# CAC Considerations

## Organic Distribution

Potential channels:
- Hacker News
- Reddit
- Indie Hackers
- Twitter/X
- founder communities

Estimated CAC:
- extremely low
- primarily time/content investment

This makes the product attractive as a product-led acquisition mechanism.

---

# Paid Acquisition Risks

Paid acquisition may become difficult if:
- users do not perceive enough savings value
- recommendations feel generic
- audits are not shareable

The product must generate:
- curiosity
- screenshots
- internal sharing

to maintain efficient acquisition economics.

---

# Path to $1M ARR

To approach:

```text
$1M ARR
```

the product would likely need:

- significantly larger inbound traffic
- stronger enterprise conversion
- recurring monitoring subscriptions
- procurement workflow integrations
- benchmarking analytics

Example scenario:

```text
500 paying companies
× $2,000 annual value
= $1,000,000 ARR
```

This becomes plausible if the tool establishes itself as:
- a trusted AI spend benchmarking platform
- a procurement optimization layer
- an AI cost visibility tool for startups

---

# Biggest Economic Risk

The largest risk is:
- users finding the audit interesting but not valuable enough to act on

This reduces:
- consultation conversion
- infrastructure credit demand
- monetization efficiency

The audit recommendations must therefore feel:
- actionable
- trustworthy
- financially meaningful
